The Revival Meeting
In spite of hot weather, busy sea­
son and multiplied demands on peo­
ple's attention, the attendance and
interest at the revival meeting nt the
Christian Church continues to grow.
A fine crowd greeted the evangelist
again Tuesday night, in what was said
to be one of the finest "mother" ser­
vices ever witnessed here. The mem­
bers of the various congregations are
attending in increasing numbers, and
helping with the music. The fellow­
ship is fine. To-night Evangelist Ear­
ley wdll preach on the subject, "The
,Church that Christ Built.’ Saturday
night the Junior Chorus will lead the
song service, and the sermon will be
appropriate to the occasion. The first
baptismal service will probably be
held on Saturday afternoon at 3:30.
The meetings will continue right along
until further announcement.
